## Key Concepts

- Phishing is a form of **social engineering** that manipulates users into revealing sensitive information or executing malicious actions.
- Unlike spam, phishing emails are specifically crafted to deceive recipients and often aim to steal credentials or deliver malware.
- A successful phishing attack can provide attackers with an initial foothold into an organization's network.
- As defenders, our responsibility is to analyze suspicious emails, identify malicious indicators, and determine whether an email is legitimate or malicious.

## Important Notes

> Spam is generally unsolicited bulk email, whereas phishing is specifically designed to deceive users into revealing sensitive information or performing harmful actions.

> A phishing investigation involves examining multiple email components rather than relying on a single indicator.

> Email analysis is a fundamental skill for SOC analysts, incident responders, and blue team defenders.
