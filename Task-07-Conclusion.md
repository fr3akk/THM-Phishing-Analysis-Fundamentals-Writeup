## Business Email Compromise (BEC)

**Business Email Compromise (BEC)** is a phishing attack in which an attacker gains access to or impersonates a legitimate business email account to deceive employees, customers, or partners into performing unauthorized actions.

Common objectives include:

- Fraudulent wire transfers
- Invoice payment scams
- Credential theft
- Theft of sensitive business information
- Financial fraud

BEC attacks are particularly dangerous because they rely on trust rather than malware, making them difficult to detect using traditional security controls.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Always verify the sender's identity.
- Inspect email headers before trusting an email.
- Be cautious of urgent requests and unexpected attachments.
- Verify hyperlinks before clicking them.
- Analyze suspicious emails in a secure environment.
- Defang malicious URLs and IP addresses when sharing indicators.
- Report phishing attempts to the organization's security team.
